Between 1959 and 1962, twenty-five women were drafted into a privately funded program to gauge whether women can handle the biological demands of going into space. They were subjected to the same battery of physical and psychological tests as the men of Mercury 7 program. Thirteen of those women passed the test and are now often referred to as the Mercury 13. The program was halted prematurely by Lyndon Johnson and NASA. Two of those women, Jerrie Cobb and Janey Hart, called for a special subcommittee hearing before Congress to change the requirements for astronauts, which had effectively excluded women from qualifying as astronauts thus far.
